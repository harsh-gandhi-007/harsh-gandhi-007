Introduction

I am currently working as a researcher in the field of generalized non-Newtonian fluids associated with flow through internal and external geometries. I have completed my doctoral thesis from the Department of mechanical Engineering, IIT Patna. My dissertation work addresses a sharp interface immersed boundary method to simulate non-Newtonian fluids in complex and moving geometries. I have enhanced an existing sharp interface immersed boundary method (IBM) based Newtonian solver to handle non-Newtonian fluid.

The solver is written in Fortran and is accelerated in GPU using Open-ACC directives. Further, I am working on non-Newtonian fluid flow through the Diseased Artery Model (carotid, Aorta) using ANSYS.

Non-Newtonian Fluid flow

Non-Newtonian fluid flow over complex and accelerating geometries is widely found in several natural and engineering processes, for example – transport of human body fluids, self-driving of micro-miniature organisms in complex liquids, processing of industrial chemicals, crude oils, food items, paper pulp, paint, diary, coal slurry, or ash transportation, etc. Computational simulation of these systems is vital for a better understanding of the complex fluid motion during these processes in order to achieve improvement in the engineering designs.

It has been noted that a large number of industrial applications handle non-Newtonian fluids like industrial suspensions, molten plastics, detergent solutions, multi-grade oils, paints, polymer solutions and several food and confectionery products etc. Our background study asserts on the importance of understanding the behavior of shear-thinning and shear-thickening fluids in such applications as there has been a scarcity of studies on flow over complex and moving boundaries.
